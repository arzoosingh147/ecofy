Non-Recyclable Items Information

Plastic Bags

Avoid putting plastic bags in the recycling bin. Many recycling facilities do not accept them. Consider using reusable bags.

Styrofoam

Styrofoam containers and packaging are typically not recyclable. Look for alternative packaging materials that are more environmentally friendly.

Broken Glass

Small shards of broken glass can be dangerous for workers in recycling facilities. Wrap broken glass carefully and dispose of it properly.

Plastic Utensils

Single-use plastic utensils are often not recyclable due to their low quality and mix of materials. Consider using reusable utensils or alternatives made from compostable materials to reduce waste.

Clothing with Metal Parts

Clothing items with embedded metal parts, such as shoes with metal soles or clothing with metal zippers, are challenging to recycle. Donate or repurpose such items to extend their life cycle.

Wax-Coated Paper

Wax-coated paper, often found in milk or juice cartons, is non-recyclable. The wax layer prevents efficient recycling. Dispose of these items in regular waste or explore alternative packaging options.

Ceramics

Ceramic items, such as dishes and mugs, are non-recyclable. They have different melting points than glass and can contaminate glass recycling. Consider donating or repurposing ceramic items.

Disposable Diapers

Disposable diapers are non-recyclable due to their complex composition of materials, including plastics and absorbent polymers. Properly dispose of them in regular waste bins.

Electronics

Many electronics, such as smartphones and laptops, contain hazardous materials and should not be placed in regular recycling bins. Recycle electronics through dedicated e-waste recycling programs.

Light Bulbs

Incandescent and compact fluorescent light bulbs contain materials that make them unsuitable for regular recycling. Dispose of them properly, following local guidelines for hazardous waste.

Medical Waste

Items like syringes, needles, and medical waste should never be placed in recycling bins. Follow local regulations for the safe disposal of medical waste to prevent contamination.

Composite Packaging

Packaging with multiple materials bonded together, like Tetra Paks, can be challenging to recycle. Check local guidelines for specific instructions on the disposal of such items.

Paint Cans

Paint cans should not be placed in regular recycling bins. Empty and dried paint cans can be disposed of with regular waste, while excess paint can be taken to hazardous waste collection facilities.

Plastic Film

Thin plastic films, such as plastic wrap and food packaging, are non-recyclable in curbside recycling programs. Look for drop-off locations that accept plastic film or reduce its use when possible.

Single-use Coffee Cups

Disposable coffee cups with a plastic lining are typically not recyclable due to the mix of materials. Consider using reusable cups or bringing your own travel mug to reduce waste.

Crisp Packets

Foil-lined crisp packets are challenging to recycle due to the mix of materials. Look for alternative packaging or participate in programs that collect such items for specialized recycling.

Plastic Cutlery

Single-use plastic cutlery is often non-recyclable. Opt for reusable or compostable alternatives to reduce the environmental impact of disposable utensils.

Napkins and Tissues

Used napkins and tissues, typically made from low-quality paper, are not suitable for recycling. Dispose of them in regular waste bins.

CDs and DVDs

Old CDs and DVDs are non-recyclable in curbside programs. Explore electronic waste recycling options or consider repurposing them for arts and crafts.

Plastic Toothbrushes

Most traditional plastic toothbrushes are non-recyclable. Switch to eco-friendly alternatives made from bamboo or other sustainable materials.

Foam Packaging

Styrofoam and other foam packaging materials are typically non-recyclable. Look for products with minimal packaging or those using recyclable alternatives.
