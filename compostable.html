Compostable Items Information

Food Scraps

Fruit and vegetable peels, coffee grounds, and eggshells are great additions to your compost bin. Avoid dairy and meat products.

Yard Waste

Grass clippings, leaves, and small branches can be composted. Avoid diseased plants and weeds with mature seeds.

Compostable Utensils

Utensils made from materials like cornstarch or bamboo are compostable. Ensure they meet composting facility requirements.

Coffee Grounds

Used coffee grounds are rich in nitrogen and make an excellent addition to compost. Sprinkle them in your compost bin to enhance the nutrient content.

Fruit Peels and Scraps

Compost fruit peels, cores, and scraps. Citrus peels, banana peels, and apple cores are all suitable for composting. Chop larger scraps into smaller pieces for faster decomposition.

Eggshells

Crushed eggshells are a valuable source of calcium for your compost. Rinse and crush them before adding to the compost bin. They also help in balancing the pH of the compost.

Vegetable Scraps

Compost vegetable scraps, including ends, peels, and unused parts. Avoid adding diseased plants or parts treated with pesticides. Chop or shred larger pieces to speed up decomposition.

Grass Clippings

After mowing your lawn, collect grass clippings for composting. They provide essential nitrogen and help create a well-balanced compost mixture. Avoid using grass treated with herbicides.

Tea Bags

Unbleached tea bags and used tea leaves are compostable. Remove any staples or labels before composting. Tea adds organic matter and nutrients to the compost pile.

Leaves and Plant Trimmings

Collect fallen leaves, plant trimmings, and small branches for composting. These materials contribute carbon to the compost pile and enhance its structure. Shred larger leaves for quicker decomposition.

Paper Towel Rolls

Empty cardboard paper towel rolls can be composted. Tear them into smaller pieces to aid in faster decomposition and enhance aeration in the compost pile.

Natural Cork

Compost natural cork from wine bottles or other sources. Cut or shred cork materials to speed up the composting process. Cork adds carbon to the compost pile.

Natural Fabrics

Compost natural fabrics made from materials like cotton or linen. Cut or tear them into smaller pieces to accelerate decomposition. Avoid fabrics treated with synthetic chemicals.

Bamboo Products

Compost items made from bamboo, such as plates, utensils, and toothbrushes. Bamboo is a fast-growing, renewable resource that breaks down easily in composting systems.

Pizza Boxes

Compost clean pizza boxes made from unbleached cardboard. Remove any food residue or grease before adding them to the compost bin. Torn boxes into smaller pieces for efficient composting.

Wooden Chopsticks

Compost wooden chopsticks made from untreated wood. Break them into smaller pieces to aid decomposition. Consider using reusable chopsticks to reduce overall waste.

Herbivore Manure

Compost manure from herbivores, such as cows or horses. Manure adds valuable nitrogen to the compost pile. Ensure it's well-aged and free from medications or harmful chemicals.
